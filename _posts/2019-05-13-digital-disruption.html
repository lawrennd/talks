--- title: "Digital Disruption" venue: "Future of Work, Chatham House" abstract: "

We look towards the future of digital disruption by considering the past of disruption, with a particular focus on the production and movement of goods. We introduce the notion of the 'smith', and consider how, by localizing the provision, or supply, a 'smith' can ensure high added value for their skills. Using analogies from pull and push supply chains, We argue that our future economy needs to include an environment where a smiths prosper. From craft coffee to craft software, to add value in a global marketplace we argue that we need to exploit localization.
